Using the 60% Figure
📊
How to cite it confidently
Framing note for Lark — not for public distribution
The framing to use
"Up to 60% of children don't achieve reading mastery by Grade 4." This is grounded in NAEP data (North America's largest standardized literacy assessment), which defines mastery as NAEP Proficient — demonstrated competency over grade-level material. In 2024, only 32% of Grade 4 students reached that bar. The 60% figure is conservative.
If challenged with Ontario EQAO numbers
"EQAO measures whether students are meeting Ontario curriculum expectations — that's the floor. NAEP Proficient measures actual reading mastery — the ability to handle what Grade 4 and beyond actually demands. Those are genuinely different bars. Even by EQAO's own measure, nearly 1 in 3 Ontario Grade 3 students isn't meeting the standard — and that's before we get to the mastery question."
The companion stat worth adding
"95% of children have the brain capacity to become strong readers. When they don't get there, the failure is almost always in the instruction — not the child." (Source: Harvard researcher Catherine Snow.) This removes shame and makes the problem feel entirely solvable — which it is.
