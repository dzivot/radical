Summer Surge Launch Plan

Channel Strategy

Goal: 20 families Window: 6 weeks Territory: Danforth / Beaches, Toronto
The core truth
20 families in 6 weeks in a tight geography is a trust and proximity play — not an awareness play. You don't need reach. You need conversion in a small pond.
Tier 1

Conversion channels

These close the sale. Every other channel feeds here.

1
School auction vouchers
Already in motion
Warm introduction at a high-trust, captive moment

This is your single best asset. A parent bidding on a Radical Reading voucher at a school auction is already self-qualifying — they care about their child's education, they're competitive, and they're in a room full of peers who see them bidding. The auction does the selling — your job is not to lose them at checkout.

The voucher redemption flow needs to be airtight: clear URL, simple onboarding, Lark's voice front and centre.

Watch Gift voucher is marked "donated to Frankland Community School" — make sure redemption is smooth for non-Frankland families too if vouchers circulate beyond the original school.
2
Direct peer referral
Word of mouth
Cheapest, highest-converting channel you have

Lark is a TDSB teacher in this exact community. That's not a marketing asset — that's a distribution network. Every family who enrolls needs to hear "tell one parent who'd get this" — not as a formal referral program, but as a natural ask baked into the onboarding.

The first 5 families are your sales team for families 6–20.

Tactical Give enrolled families the flyer as a digital card they can share. Make it embarrassingly easy to forward.
Tier 2

Warm traffic channels

These move people from "heard of it" to "checking it out."

3
Danforth/Beaches Facebook parent groups
Community credibility + qualified clicks to the Summer Surge product page

These groups — East End Parents, Beaches Moms/Dads, school-specific parent groups — are where your exact customer spends 20 minutes a day. The play is not ad-style posts. It's Lark showing up as a knowledgeable community member — answering questions about reading, mentioning what she sees in schools — with the program referenced naturally.

One well-placed authentic post from a credentialed local educator lands differently than any ad you could run.

Voice Lark's, not the brand's. "I'm a literacy interventionist at a TDSB school and we just launched something for summer…" beats "Radical Reading Co. is excited to announce…" every single time.
4
Instagram (local organic)
Social proof and brand legitimacy for anyone who googles you after hearing the name

When a parent hears about Radical Reading at a school auction, the first thing they do is Instagram-stalk you. Your feed needs to answer: Is this real? Is Lark credible? Do other parents trust this? That's the only job it has right now. You're not growing an audience — you're passing a vibe check.

Stories during the 6-week window can do light urgency work ("8 spots left" if true). Reels with Lark speaking directly would be powerful but aren't required to hit 20.

5
Physical flyers — hyperlocal drops
Ambient awareness that makes word-of-mouth land faster

The Summer Surge flyer is strong. Strategic placements: Danforth coffee shops (Remarkable Bean, Jimmy's, Dark Horse), indie kids' bookstores (Mabel's Fables is close), community centres, and the physical bulletin boards that still exist at schools. These don't convert cold — they prime recognition so when someone hears the name socially, it clicks.

Tactical Don't spray. Pick 8–10 hyper-relevant spots and refresh mid-campaign.
Tier 3

Long build channels

Won't hit your 20 this summer — but plant seeds that matter after August.

6
The Radical Insider blog
SEO capture for "summer reading program Toronto," "Lexia for kids Canada," "dyslexia help Ontario"

You won't rank in 6 weeks — Google doesn't work that way. But posts written now will be indexed by September and will matter for the fall enrollment cycle. The Summer Surge launch is a strong editorial moment.

First post: "Why Your Kid's School Has Lexia But Won't Give You Access to It" — pure Radical voice and designed to travel.

7
Nextdoor (Beaches / Danforth)
Hyperlocal trust signal, secondary to Facebook groups

Lower engagement ceiling than the Facebook groups but worth one well-crafted post from Lark as a local educator. Tends to attract slightly older, more homeowner-type parents — which skews toward your audience.

Not yet

Save these for the fall

The conditions aren't right yet. Running these now costs money and risks looking thin.

✕
Paid Meta / Google Ads
The unit economics at $199 CAD with a small audience size don't support meaningful paid spend in a 6-week local window. Save the ad budget for the fall enrollment push when you have testimonials, a proven product, and a broader geography. Running ads now without social proof risks looking thin.
✕
TikTok
Not where Danforth parents of JK–Grade 3 kids are making $199 decisions. Maybe Lark builds there later as an educator-creator play. Not now.

The honest math

If the 6 auction vouchers convert at even 50%, you're at 3 families.

Word-of-mouth from those 3 plus Lark's direct Facebook group presence should get you to 10–12.

The flyers and Instagram close the gap to 20 by making the brand feel established when someone goes to check.

That's the funnel. Every channel has one job. None of them work without the others.
